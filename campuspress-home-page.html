About Me

I am an Assistant Professor (Senior Lecturer) at the School of Public Health, University of Haifa, and an Adjunct Assistant Professor at the Department of Psychiatry, Yale School of Medicine. I head the Ben-Zion Resilience & Recovery Lab.

With a multidisciplinary background in biology, psychology, and neuroscience, my research investigates the neurocognitive mechanisms underlying stress vulnerability and resilience, with the goal of informing novel treatments for stress- and anxiety-related disorders, particularly post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).

My work integrates behavioral, cognitive, physiological, and neural measures using advanced statistical and computational methods, grounded in a commitment to transparent, reproducible science and enriched by extensive interdisciplinary collaboration.

Research Directions

1. Neural computations of stress and anxiety — How neural computations of value and uncertainty shape learning, memory, and decision-making in the context of rewards and punishments, and how alterations in these computations relate to anxiety and stress-related symptoms.

2. Theory-driven process-based PTSD subtypes — Investigating PTSD subtypes using objective neurobehavioral measures to improve diagnostic accuracy and optimize treatment outcomes.

3. Computational psychiatry and AI — Applying tools from computational psychiatry to explore the behavior of large language models (e.g., ChatGPT), generating insights into human learning mechanisms and mental health applications.
